## Recommended Day-Trip Itinerary: The Mirabello Circuit

This itinerary maximizes the morning cool for historical exploration and reserves the heat of the afternoon for refreshing swims.

* **08:30 AM | Departure to Plaka:** Head north from Mavrikiano to Plaka. Arriving early ensures you beat the midday heat and the larger tour groups.
* **09:00 AM | Spinalonga Exploration:** Catch one of the first boats from Plaka to Spinalonga. Spend 1.5 to 2 hours wandering the stone pathways, absorbing the island's formidable history.
* **11:30 AM | Seaside Lunch in Plaka:** Return to Plaka and choose a traditional waterfront taverna. Enjoy freshly caught seafood with an uninterrupted view of the fortress you just explored.
* **01:30 PM | Afternoon Swim at Plaka Beach:** Cool off in the crystal-clear waters of the pebble beach right next to the village.
* **03:00 PM | Sunken City of Olous:** Drive south through Elounda to the isthmus. Park near the windmills and snorkel over the submerged ruins of the ancient city.
* **04:30 PM | Retreat to Kolokitha Beach:** Brave the rugged dirt road (or take the scenic hike over the hill) to this hidden, sandy cove. The late afternoon light here is spectacular, and the waters are usually calm and shallow.
* **07:00 PM | Return to Mavrikiano:** Head back to your base to refresh before enjoying a traditional Cretan dinner under the stars.

## Essential Packing List

To fully enjoy the varied terrain and environments around Elounda, pack these essentials:

* **Footwear for varied terrain:** Sturdy walking sandals or sneakers (essential for the uneven stones of Spinalonga and the hike to Kolokitha).
* **Water shoes:** Highly recommended for getting in and out of the water comfortably at Plaka's pebble beach and around the rocky ruins of Olous.
* **Snorkeling gear:** A must-have for observing the underwater mosaics and stones at the Sunken City of Olous.
* **Sun protection:** The Cretan sun is unforgiving. Bring a wide-brimmed hat, polarized sunglasses, and reef-safe sunscreen.
* **Hydration & Snacks:** Bring plenty of water, especially for the hike over the Kolokitha peninsula where there are no facilities.
* **Light layers:** A lightweight, breathable long-sleeve shirt or cover-up for boat rides and intense midday sun.
* **Dry bag:** To protect your phone, wallet, and keys from sea spray on the boat to Spinalonga or while relaxing on the beaches.

## Spinalonga Visitor Information (2026 Season)

### Operating Hours (Summer Season: April 1 - October 31)
The archaeological site is open daily from **08:30 AM to 6:00 PM** (18:00).
* The last admission is typically 30 minutes before closing.
* *Note: The site is closed to the general public during the winter months (November 1st - March 31st).*

### Site Entrance Tickets
You will purchase your entrance tickets at the ticket office immediately upon disembarking on the island.
* **Full Price:** €20
* **Reduced Price:** €10 (Seniors over 65)
* **Free Admission:** Children, young people, and students under 25 (EU citizens).

### Boat Transfers
Boats operate continuously throughout the day starting around 8:30 AM or 9:00 AM, aligning with the site's opening hours. There is no need to book these small transfers in advance; you can simply arrive at the docks.

#### From Plaka
This is the shortest and most convenient crossing.
* **Ticket Price:** €10 to €12 for a round trip.
* **Travel Time:** About 7 to 10 minutes.
* **Frequency:** Every 30 minutes.

#### From Schisma Eloundas
A slightly longer, scenic route across the bay.
* **Ticket Price:** €12 to €14 for a round trip.
* **Travel Time:** About 20 to 25 minutes.
* **Frequency:** Every 30 minutes.

> **Pro Tip:** Driving or walking up to Plaka to catch the first boats around 8:30 AM or 9:00 AM is highly recommended. You will experience the fortress before the midday heat sets in and before the large tour boats arrive from Agios Nikolaos.
