House Argyro — Website Description
Short introduction

House Argyro is a traditional Cretan stone home located in the historic village of Mavrikiano above Elounda Bay. Built in 1905 and carefully preserved through generations, the house combines authentic village architecture with simple modern comfort. From its veranda and upper bedroom the view opens across the blue waters of the Gulf of Elounda, while the quiet alleys of the village surround the house with a sense of calm and continuity.

The space

The house unfolds across several levels and offers 85 square metres of interior space designed for up to four guests. Thick stone walls keep the interior naturally cool in summer and warm during the mild Cretan winter. The two bedrooms are arranged to provide privacy and views toward the sea and the village rooftops.

The living areas reflect the character of traditional homes in eastern Crete: simple proportions, natural materials and a layout that connects interior rooms with outdoor spaces.

Outdoor living

A sea-view veranda forms the heart of the house. From here guests can watch the morning light over Elounda Bay or enjoy the quiet evenings that settle over the village.

The private courtyard provides an additional outdoor area with an open kitchenette and barbecue, making it easy to prepare simple meals outside and enjoy the slower rhythm of village life.

Location and surroundings

House Argyro sits in Mavrikiano, one of the historic settlements above Elounda. The village retains the character of traditional Cretan architecture while remaining close to the coast.

From the house it is roughly a 10-minute walk down to Elounda, where guests will find beaches, waterfront tavernas and small shops. The elevated position of the village provides open views across the bay while keeping the atmosphere quieter than the seafront.

The experience

Staying in House Argyro is less about luxury and more about living inside the landscape of Elounda. Stone walls, shaded courtyards and sea views connect the house to the rhythms of the village and the surrounding hills.

For travelers who want to experience Crete beyond the resort hotels, the house offers a simple and authentic base from which to explore the region.
