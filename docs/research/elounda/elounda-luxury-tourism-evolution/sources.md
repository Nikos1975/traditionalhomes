# **The Metamorphosis of Elounda: From Post-Colonial Deprivation to the Vanguard of Branded Eco-Luxury**

## **Invention of Luxury Tourism**

In the immediate aftermath of the leper colony's closure, the Elounda region plunged into a severe economic depression. Deprived of the state funding, the administrative salaries, and the robust supply chains required to maintain the massive facility on Spinalonga, the mainland settlements of Schisma, Plaka, and Mavrikiano rapidly regressed into quiet, deeply impoverished fishing villages. However, this period of obscurity was remarkably brief. The region was poised for a final, spectacular metamorphosis, propelled by global media exposure and the visionary development of high-end, exclusive tourism.

## **The Cinematic Discovery of Mirabello**

The aesthetic of untouched, rugged beauty—an aesthetic ironically preserved by decades of poverty and the terrifying stigma of the leprosy colony—suddenly caught the eye of the international film industry in the 1960s. In 1964, Walt Disney Productions selected the pristine, undeveloped backdrops of Elounda and nearby Agios Nikolaos to film the mystery-adventure movie *The Moon-Spinners*. Starring Hayley Mills, Eli Wallach, and Pola Negri, the film utilized the raw romance of the Cretan landscape, introducing the dramatic topography of the Mirabello Bay to an international audience.  
This initial cinematic exposure was massively amplified a decade later by the British Broadcasting Corporation. In 1977, the BBC produced the highly successful, eight-episode television drama *Who Pays the Ferryman?*. Created and written by Michael J. Bird, the series tapped deeply into the local history, mythology, and folklore of the island. The plot followed Alan Haldane (known locally by his andarte resistance name, Leandros), a former British soldier who returns to Crete thirty years after fighting alongside the local resistance during WWII, only to discover he has a daughter named Elena running a struggling taverna in Elounda.  
Filmed extensively on location in and around Elounda, the series showcased the breathtaking coastal scenery and the authentic culture of the local inhabitants. Bolstered by an iconic, lyrical musical score composed by Yannis Markopoulos, the series became a massive cultural phenomenon in Britain. It broadcast the dramatic, sun-drenched beauty of the region directly into millions of European living rooms, firmly planting Elounda on the cultural map and initiating a tidal wave of curious, affluent travelers eager to experience the romanticized setting.

## **Spyros Kokotos and the Architecture of the Jet Set**

While television and film provided the exposure, the infrastructure required to transform Elounda from a quaint fishing village into the "St. Tropez of Crete" was masterminded by the visionary Greek architect Spyros Kokotos and his wife, Eleni. Identifying the unparalleled potential of the Mirabello coastline, Kokotos pioneered a completely revolutionary model of hospitality in Greece.  
During the 1970s, the prevailing model for Mediterranean tourism relied heavily on brutalist, high-rise concrete hotel blocks designed for cheap, mass-market package holidays. Kokotos vehemently rejected this approach. Instead, he championed a philosophy of low-impact, hyper-luxurious architecture that integrated seamlessly with the natural topography, utilizing local materials and respecting traditional Cretan aesthetic forms. In 1971, he designed and completed the Minos Beach hotel just north of Agios Nikolaos.1 Widely recognized upon its opening as the most luxurious hotel in Greece, Minos Beach proved that a massive, untapped market existed for elite, high-end accommodations.1  
Capitalizing on this success, Kokotos turned his attention directly to Elounda. In 1977, he completed the Elounda Bay Palace, followed shortly by the legendary Elounda Beach Hotel.1 These resorts introduced unprecedented, opulent amenities to the Greek market. Kokotos continuously expanded the properties, introducing the first thalassotherapy spa in Elounda (the Thermais), inventing the concept of private seafront bungalows with individual, temperature-controlled pools, and later designing the Elounda Peninsula All Suite Hotel, which featured Crete's only private resort golf course.1

By catering exclusively to the international jet set—regularly hosting Hollywood royalty, global politicians, and extremely wealthy international dignitaries—Kokotos completely eradicated Elounda’s historical stigma of poverty and disease, rebranding the region as the absolute zenith of Mediterranean exclusivity. His influence on the national economy was so profound that he became a founding member and the first president of the Association of Greek Tourism Enterprises (SETE), delivering a seminal 13-page speech that successfully united the nation's professional tourism associations to advocate for cohesive, high-quality government tourism policies.

## **The Transition to Corporate Hospitality and Family Dynasties (1980s–2000s)**

The pioneering low-density, high-luxury model introduced by Spyros Kokotos laid the structural foundation for the next stage of Elounda's development. As the international tourism industry matured through the late 1980s and into the 2000s, the localized, single-asset hotel model transitioned into an era of family-owned corporate hospitality dynasties.1 During this period, three prominent Greek families—the Kokotos, Sbokos, and Spanos families—systematically acquired premium coastal real estate, introducing sophisticated brand hierarchies that divided the Mirabello coastline into distinct pockets of exclusive luxury.1

### **The Kokotos Family and the Elounda Collection Expansion**

Spyros and Eliana Kokotos continuously strived to improve and expand their portfolio, systematically acquiring land and building new facilities year after year.1 This focused, long-term ownership model allowed the family to preserve the ultra-premium identity of their developments.1  
In 1982, the family opened the **Elounda Mare Hotel**, the first property entirely owned by the family.1 It made history as the first hotel in the Mediterranean to feature private pools for each of its bungalows, establishing a new trend for luxury resorts.1 In 1987, its status was globally cemented when it became the first Greek hotel to join the prestigious Relais & Châteaux association, leveraging its focus on charm, calm, and culinary distinction.1  
Building on this success, the family completed the **Porto Elounda Golf & Spa Resort** in 1992\.1 Designed to capture emerging trends in the international luxury market, Porto Elounda introduced the island's first resort golf course and pioneered the region’s first hotel-based time-share ownership scheme, allowing affluent travelers to secure fractional ownership of beachfront villas.1 This period also saw the introduction of the "Thermais" spa, the first dedicated wellness center of its kind in Elounda.1  
In 2002, the family launched the **Elounda Peninsula All Suite Hotel**.1 Designed exclusively as an all-suite hotel, it offered the largest private-pool seafront suites in Greece, integrating specialized guest amenities such as a private cinema, an expansive wine cellar, and the acclaimed Calypso gourmet restaurant.1  
Partnering with the globally renowned wellness brand Six Senses, the family developed an expansive spa facility in 2006, which was named the "World's Best Spa" by the *Sunday Times' Travel* magazine.1 This spa was instrumental in facilitating year-round operations, making the Elounda Collection the only hospitality provider in the area to remain open 12 months a year since 1997\.1 The family's latest project is the **Elounda Peninsula Diamond Residences**, a seafront wing dedicated to private holiday homes where owners enjoy direct beach access while the resort manages property maintenance and hospitality services.1

### **Yiannis Sbokos and the Conception of Blue Palace**

While the Kokotos family dominated the central Elounda coastline, the visionary engineer Yiannis Sbokos established the Sbokos Hotel Group in 1976\.4 In the late 1980s, Sbokos acquired a sprawling, dramatically sloped plot of land directly opposite the historic islet of Spinalonga, near the fishing village of Plaka.2  
Sbokos constructed the **Blue Palace**, which opened in 2003\.2 The design utilized locally excavated pink-hued stone and earthy terracotta plaster applied via the traditional Cretan *kourassani* technique, allowing the low-rise buildings to blend into the rugged topography.2 In 2004, the property joined Marriott’s Luxury Collection, establishing the sleepy fishing village of Plaka as a premium destination capable of competing with Elounda's established center.2  
In 2017, ownership transitioned to Sbokos's daughters, Agapi and Costantza Sbokou.5 In 2019, the Sbokos Hotel Group was rebranded as **PHĀEA** (or Phāea Resorts), embarking on a major corporate restructuring.4 Backed by an alliance with Eftichios Vassilakis (Chairman of Aegean Airlines), PHĀEA repositioned its portfolio to focus on high-concept, eco-conscious, and culturally authentic luxury, laying the groundwork for some of the most capital-intensive redevelopments in modern Greek history.4

### **The Spanos Family and the Rise of Domes Resorts**

In parallel with the Sbokos family, the Spanos family, operating through Ledra Hotels & Villas, began executing a highly aggressive regional expansion plan.3 The family's real estate strategy focused on acquiring underperforming coastal assets and systematically upgrading them into ultra-premium lifestyle properties.3  
The family entered the Elounda market with the acquisition of Elounda Blu Bay, which they systematically expanded and upgraded from a basic three-star property to a refined four-star resort.3 This was accompanied by the opening of the Pleiades Luxurious Villas in 2003, capturing the growing demand for private, residential-style vacation rentals with full resort services.3  
In 2008, the Spanos family launched their flagship brand, **Domes of Elounda**.3 Featuring 158 luxurious suites and villas, the resort distinguished itself through striking contemporary architecture, a private sandy beach, and family-oriented luxury programming.3 The property was later expanded with the addition of the "CORE"—an innovative open-air cultural and lifestyle hub integrating art, fashion, gastronomy, and wellness.3

## **The Era of Institutional Financialization and Asset-Light Alliances (2010s–2020s)**

By the mid-2010s, the global hospitality industry was undergoing a structural shift toward "asset-light" operational models. Rather than carrying massive real estate portfolios on their balance sheets, developers sought to unlock value by partnering with institutional private equity funds and international hotel chains.3 Elounda became a primary target for this institutionalization, transitioning from family-owned and operated independent hotels to a highly financialized ecosystem.3

### **The Domes-Marriott Alliance**

The first major manifestation of this institutional shift occurred in 2015, when Domes of Elounda became the first property in Greece to join Marriott International's Autograph Collection.3 This alliance granted the Spanos family unprecedented access to Marriott’s global distribution channels and loyalty network while preserving the boutique, highly localized character of the Domes brand.3  
Recognizing the scalability of this model, Domes expanded rapidly.12 In 2016, the family opened the Domes Noruz Chania as an Autograph Collection member.3 In 2018, they signed an agreement to operate the historic **Domes Miramare** on Corfu island under Marriott's Luxury Collection brand, restoring a legacy property originally built for the Onassis family in the late 1960s.3 This was followed by the transition of Casa Cook Chania into the **Domes Zeen Chania**, also operated under The Luxury Collection.3

### **Blackstone and Hotel Investment Partners’ Recapitalization**

Recognizing the immense value-creation opportunities in the southern European resort sector, Hotel Investment Partners (HIP)—the largest owner of resort hotels in Southern Europe, owned by funds managed by Blackstone—entered the Greek market.10 In 2020 and 2021, HIP signed a series of agreements to acquire major Greek resort assets from local groups, including Elounda Blu, Grand Hotel on Corfu, and Zante Beach Resort on Zakynthos.10  
This transaction established a highly lucrative corporate synergy: HIP assumed real estate ownership, injecting over €6 million of capital to completely renovate, expand, and upgrade Elounda Blu into a five-star internationally branded resort, while the Spanos family’s Domes Resorts retained long-term management rights.10 This recapitalization culminated in the property's dramatic rebirth in 2022 as the **Domes Aulūs Elounda** (operating under Hilton’s Curio Collection), an adults-only, ultra-all-inclusive resort featuring the exclusive "Cool Living Selection" VIP tier.3

### **The Expansion of Sandglass and Everty**

The institutionalization of Elounda's hospitality real estate was further advanced by the entry of Everty Services Limitada, an international real estate investment firm based in Costa Rica.17 Operating as part of a €100 million ongoing investment strategy in Greece, Everty acquired the legendary **Elounda Gulf Villas**.18  
The property, consisting of 33 luxury suites and pool villas overlooking the Mirabello Gulf, was integrated into Everty’s hospitality division, **Sandglass Hotels & Villas Collection**.18 By transition to the Sandglass platform, the resort retained its intimate, family-run character (which earned it the \#3 spot in Greece’s Best Resorts in Condé Nast Traveler's Readers' Choice Awards) while leveraging Everty’s international asset management and capital structures.18

## **The Modern Era: Branded Residences and Master-Planned Megaprojects (Mid-2020s–2030)**

As Elounda entered the mid-2020s, the definition of luxury tourism underwent a profound transformation. The traditional model of isolated, seasonal hotels was largely superseded by massive, multi-million-euro mixed-use developments that integrated world-class hospitality with branded residential real estate, deep-water marinas, and holistic wellness ecosystems.7 These megaprojects are designed to appeal directly to ultra-high-net-worth (UHNW) buyers seeking permanent lifestyle sanctuaries, often leveraging investment incentives such as the Greek Golden Visa program.7

### **Mirum Group's Elounda Hills Megaproject**

The most structurally significant development in the modern history of Crete is **Elounda Hills**, a massive, master-planned sustainable community developed by the international real estate firm Mirum Group.21 Founded and chaired by developer Vitaly Borisov, Mirum Group has invested heavily in northeastern Crete, establishing a highly ambitious project that occupies approximately 95 hectares (950 stremmas) of elevated coastal terrain overlooking Mirabello Bay.22

* **The 1 Hotels Alliance:** Spanning a 138-acre footprint, the resort is anchored by the first European property of Barry Sternlicht’s nature-inspired luxury lifestyle brand, **1 Hotels & Homes**.21 The accommodation layout is divided into two distinct nodes: the Hilltop 1 Hotel & Homes (featuring 129 keys) and a highly exclusive Marina Hotel (comprising 15 luxury suites).30  
* **Crete's First Branded Residences:** Elounda Hills introduces a level of branded real estate previously unseen on the island, offering over 600 privately owned apartments, townhouses, and customizable villas.23 These include the Hilltop Residences, the Terrace Villas, the Marina Collection, and seven ultra-premium Waterfront Villas designed by Makridis Associates, featuring bespoke interiors curated by Ralph Lauren Home.21  
* **The Camper & Nicholsons Marina:** Slated to open in phases between 2026 and 2028, the project features a state-of-the-art, private marina managed by the prestigious yachting brand Camper & Nicholsons.21 This marina is designed to accommodate mega-yachts, establishing Elounda as a premier maritime hub in the southern Aegean.23  
* **Sustainable Infrastructure and Well-being:** Designed in accordance with strict BREEAM and LEED practices, the entire resort incorporates biophilic design principles.21 The structures are powered by renewable energy sources, and the landscape features indigenous flora, botanical gardens, and olive canopies.21 Wellness is anchored by a luxury spa operated by Bamford Wellness, complemented by a retail piazza, nine dining venues, two private beach clubs, and a 2-kilometer seafront promenade.21  
* **Construction and Contracting:** Construction on Elounda Hills officially commenced in Q1 2022\.30 To optimize execution, Mirum Group introduced the innovative Early Contractor Involvement (ECI) model to the Greek market, signing agreements with major construction firms AKTOR S.A. and Domiki Kritis S.A..22 Phase 1 construction is designed to peak between 2027 and 2028, with full project completion targeted before 2030\.22

### **PHĀEA and the Rosewood Blue Palace Transformation**

Not to be outdone by greenfield megaprojects, PHĀEA, led by Agapi and Costantza Sbokou alongside Eftichios Vassilakis, is executing a massive €100 million redevelopment of their flagship Plaka estate.7

Scheduled to reopen in 2027, the legendary Blue Palace is being transformed into an integrated tourist complex of 250 acres.7 A major section of this development will debut as the **Rosewood Blue Palace**, marking the entry of the ultra-luxury Rosewood Hotels & Resorts brand into the Greek market.7

* **Architectural Re-imagination:** Designed by the acclaimed Greek architectural firm K-Studio, the resort will feature 154 highly refined rooms and suites, 85 of which will feature private plunge pools.34 The interiors of the six restaurants and bars are being created by the internationally renowned designer Afroditi Krassa, aiming to blend contemporary aesthetics with deep Cretan cultural narratives.34

* **Green Energy Engineering:** To ensure long-term environmental sustainability, the resort’s design integrates efficient water-management systems and a powerful 999.9 kW photovoltaic solar array, significantly reducing the carbon footprint of this sprawling coastal estate.7

### **PHĀEA's Broader Regional Expansion**

PHĀEA's aggressive growth strategy is not limited to Elounda. The group is expanding its premium portfolio across the Greek islands, establishing new strategic milestones.33  
In South Crete, at the "Skouros" location in Viannos, PHĀEA is developing the **PHĀEA South Crete** project.8 Spanning 155 acres and backed by a €121.11 million investment, this model holiday resort will feature a 140-room five-star hotel, 30 autonomous tourist residences, a modern wellness spa, and extensive organic agriculture.8 Its proximity to the upcoming Kastelli International Airport (38 km) positions it perfectly on the global map of high-end destinations.8  
Additionally, PHĀEA received a building permit in November 2025 to construct a new five-star hotel complex in Plathiena, Milos.33 The project will offer 96 beds and feature low-rise, traditional stone architecture, underground spaces, and a low environmental footprint, establishing the group at the forefront of Milos’ transition into a premier, high-income destination.33

## **Independent Eco-Luxury, Bioclimatic Design, and Contemporary Lifestyle Concepts (2020–2026)**

While massive, master-planned communities dominate the headlines, a parallel development era has emerged in the 2020s: the rise of highly specialized, independent five-star boutique hotels.35 These properties reject the sprawling footprint of megaprojects, choosing instead to focus on architectural innovation, bioclimatic engineering, and experience-led lifestyle concepts.35

### **Cayo Exclusive Resort & Spa**

Developed by Cayo SA (Energetiki Techniki Anaptixiaki AE) and completed across phases in 2020 and 2023/2024, Cayo Exclusive Resort & Spa sits on a steep, dramatically sloped plot in Plaka, directly overlooking Spinalonga.38

* **Bioclimatic Engineering:** The resort's architecture utilizes a stepped, terraced design that integrates into the natural mountainside.36 To optimize thermal efficiency, Cayo features advanced bioclimatic systems, including a state-of-the-art heat recovery system that captures waste heat from the massive air-conditioning networks to heat the main building's water supply.38  
* **Bespoke Interior Design:** The interior and exterior spaces were designed by the famous Italian art director Gian Paolo Venier.36 Venier combined clean, modern lines with traditional Cretan materials, ensuring that all 116 rooms and suites feature their own private swimming pools and unobstructed sea views.36  
* **Gastronomic Distinction:** Cayo's culinary program is curated by the legendary, Michelin-starred Greek chef Lefteris Lazarou, presenting contemporary, elevated interpretations of traditional Cretan and Mediterranean recipes across four distinct restaurants, including the fine-dining venue Kelari.36

### **Royal Marmin Bay Boutique & Art Hotel**

Operating as an adults-only sanctuary overlooking Mirabello Bay, the Royal Marmin Bay Boutique & Art Hotel achieved significant recognition as the **first energy-efficient, ecologically green hotel in Greece**.37

* **Green Tech and Autonomous Rooms:** The resort's construction utilizes smart architectural choices and advanced insulation to create energy-autonomous rooms, minimizing electrical grid reliance.37  
* **Pure Organic Gastronomy:** Reflecting its green philosophy, the hotel's culinary outlets use organic produce sourced from local farmers, with extra virgin olive oil utilized exclusively for all culinary preparations.37  
* **Ultra-Premium Amenities:** Despite its strict ecological constraints, the resort maintains world-class luxury services, including a private beach, a luxury spa, three fine-dining venues (including the Samurai Sushi Bar & Restaurant and Poseidon Creative Cuisine), and a private helipad.43

### **INNSiDE by Meliá Elounda**

Marking a major milestone in the democratization of five-star luxury, the **INNSiDE by Meliá Elounda** opened its doors in May 2026\.35 Developed and owned by the Crete-based Zeus Hotels in a strategic partnership with Spain's Meliá Hotels International, this resort represents the complete renovation and repositioning of the former Elounda Ilion.35

* **The Lifestyle Concept:** This property represents the debut of the lifestyle-focused INNSiDE by Meliá brand in the Greek Islands, offering a more accessible, experience-led approach to five-star hospitality.35  
* **Interactive Design:** The resort features 84 to 86 contemporary rooms and suites, with approximately 70% offering direct sea views and 18 units boasting private plunge pools.35 Its layout emphasizes social interaction, featuring Open Living Lounges that merge the lobby, reception, and bar, alongside a creative "Big Idea Space" designed for collaborative social gatherings.47  
* **Contemporary Wellness and Pet-Friendly Policy:** Wellness is integrated through outdoor yoga decks, an open-air fitness studio, and in-room lifestyle touches like yoga mats and Chromecast-equipped systems.35 Reflecting modern traveler demographics, it operates as a fully dog-friendly resort, providing customized pet beds and dining bowls in-room.46

## **Strategic Outlook and Nuanced Conclusions**

The structural trajectory of hotel development in Elounda, spanning over five decades, reveals a highly sophisticated transition from independent, family-led pioneering efforts to an era of intense institutional financialization, global brand alignment, and master-planned mixed-use communities.1  
A major force driving this transition is the economic imperative of de-risking high-yield hospitality assets.7 The historical model of relying solely on transient, seasonal hotel bookings left massive capital investments highly vulnerable to winter closures and fluctuations in regional tour-operator control.1 By integrating "branded residences" into projects like Elounda Hills and Rosewood Blue Palace, developers can secure substantial upfront liquidity via off-plan sales.7 This model also attracts permanent or semi-permanent residents who remain active in the local economy year-round, successfully mitigating the seasonal volatility that historically defined Mediterranean luxury tourism.1  
Furthermore, the integration of global distribution engines has structurally repositioned local operators.3 Local dynasties, such as the Spanos family (Domes Resorts) and the Sbokou family (PHĀEA), have recognized that maintaining long-term competitiveness requires partnering with global powerhouses like Marriott, Hilton, or Rosewood.3 These alliances grant local properties instant access to global customer databases and loyalty networks, enabling them to bypass online travel agency (OTA) intermediaries, maximize Average Daily Rates (ADRs), and appeal to a highly mobile, global UHNW demographic.3  
Finally, the long-term viability of Elounda’s multi-million-euro hospitality ecosystem is structurally safeguarded by systemic regional infrastructure upgrades.8 The upcoming opening of the Kastelli International Airport (slated for 2027/2028)—featuring state-of-the-art private jet terminals and located less than 40 minutes from Elounda and Viannos—will bypass the capacity limitations of Heraklion Airport.8 This dramatic expansion of high-end direct accessibility will accelerate the transition of northeastern Crete from a transient seasonal holiday enclave to a world-class, permanent destination for international lifestyle integration and wealth preservation.8
